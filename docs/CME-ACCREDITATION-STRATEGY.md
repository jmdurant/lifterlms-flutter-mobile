# CME Accreditation Strategy & Commercialization Guide

## Overview

This document outlines the strategy for obtaining CME (Continuing Medical Education) accreditation for courses generated by the LifterLMS AI-powered platform, leveraging faculty positions at Duke University School of Nursing and UNC School of Medicine.

---

## Faculty Positions & Accreditation Paths

| Institution | School | Accrediting Body | Credit Type | Target Audience |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|-------------|-----------------|
| UNC | School of Medicine | ACCME | AMA PRA Category 1 | Physicians (MD/DO), PAs |
| Duke | School of Nursing | ANCC | Contact Hours | RNs, NPs, CNS |

Both institutions hold ACCME/ANCC accreditation with commendation (highest status). As faculty, activities can be proposed directly through their respective CME offices without needing external joint providership.

### Dual Accreditation Advantage

Courses can carry **both** AMA PRA Category 1 credits (via UNC) **and** ANCC Contact Hours (via Duke) simultaneously. This is known as joint accreditation and significantly increases market reach.

**Combined addressable market:**

| Profession | US Licensed Providers | Annual CME Required |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Physicians (MD/DO) | ~1,100,000 | 20-50 hours/year |
| Registered Nurses | ~4,700,000 | 15-30 hours/year |
| Nurse Practitioners | ~355,000 | 25-50 hours/year |
| Physician Assistants | ~168,000 | 50 hours/year |
| **Total** | **~6,300,000+** | |

## Accreditation Process

### Step 1: Institutional CME Office Engagement

**UNC School of Medicine — Office of CME**
- Handles ACCME accreditation for physician credits
- Supports enduring materials (self-paced online) format
- Faculty submit activity proposals through their internal portal
- Contact: UNC Office of Continuing Medical Education

**Duke University School of Nursing — Office of CNE**
- Handles ANCC accreditation for nursing credits
- Supports enduring materials format
- Faculty-initiated activity proposal process
- Contact: Duke School of Nursing Continuing Education Office

### Step 2: Activity Proposal Submission

Each course requires:
1. **Needs assessment** — evidence that the educational gap exists (literature, practice data)
2. **Learning objectives** — measurable, mapped to the identified gap
3. **Target audience** — which provider types the course is designed for
4. **Content outline** — module/lesson structure with topics
5. **Faculty disclosures** — conflict of interest declarations
6. **Assessment strategy** — how learner competence is measured (quizzes, post-tests)
7. **Evaluation plan** — how the activity's effectiveness will be measured
8. **Commercial support disclosure** — if any (ideally none)

### Step 3: Peer Review

- Clinical content reviewed by a qualified peer reviewer (typically a colleague in the same specialty)
- Reviewer checks for: clinical accuracy, evidence basis, balance, absence of commercial bias
- Timeline: typically 1-2 weeks per course

### Step 4: CME Office Approval

- CME office verifies ACCME/ANCC compliance
- Assigns credit hours based on content length and complexity
- Sets up credit reporting codes
- Timeline: days to 1 week once peer review is complete

### Step 5: Publication & Credit Tracking

- Course published on the platform
- Learner completions tracked via LifterLMS
- Credits reported to ACCME/ANCC per their requirements
- Certificates of completion generated with credit information

## Approval Timeline Estimates

### Recommended Approach: Platform-Level Framework Approval

Rather than submitting each course individually, seek approval of the **platform framework** as a program of enduring materials.

**What gets reviewed once (upfront):**
- Disclosure/COI collection process
- Course template structure (objectives → content → assessment → evaluation)
- Learner evaluation mechanism
- Credit assignment methodology
- Completion and credit tracking system

**After framework approval, each new course only requires:**
- SME/peer review of clinical content (1-2 weeks)
- CME office sign-off (days)

| Phase | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Initial meeting with CME offices | Week 1 |
| Platform framework review | Weeks 2-8 |
| First batch of courses through peer review | Weeks 4-10 (can overlap) |
| First courses live with CME credit | ~2-3 months from start |
| Each subsequent course after framework approval | 1-2 weeks |

### Alternative: Individual Activity Submission

- 4-8 weeks per course
- Manageable for 1-5 courses
- Does not scale — each course needs its own full review cycle
- Not recommended for the AI-generated course model

## Commercialization Options

### Option 1: License Through University Tech Transfer

**Duke Office of Licensing & Ventures (OLV) / UNC Office of Technology Commercialization (OTC)**

- University helps commercialize the platform
- They take an equity or royalty cut
- Provides institutional backing and legal protection
- Slower, more bureaucratic process

**Pros:** Institutional support, legal cover, brand association
**Cons:** Loss of control, revenue share, slow decision-making

### Option 2: Separate Company with Joint Providership Agreement (Recommended)

Form an independent LLC/company, then enter a **joint providership agreement** with Duke and/or UNC CME offices.

- You provide the platform and content
- They provide accreditation
- Clear separation between commercial entity and institution
- Standard model used by hundreds of commercial CME companies
- Your faculty status provides the relationship to make this happen

**Pros:** IP stays yours, full business control, standard industry model
**Cons:** Need to establish the joint providership formally

**Typical joint providership terms:**
- Revenue share: 15-25% to the accrediting institution (academic centers tend toward higher end)
- Per-learner fee model also possible: $5-15 per credit hour per learner
- Some use hybrid: small admin fee ($500-1,500) + smaller revenue share (5-10%)
- No upfront cost in most arrangements

**What the accrediting institution provides for their cut:**
- ACCME/ANCC accreditation (you don't need your own)
- Peer review coordination
- Credit reporting to ACCME/AMA/ANCC
- Learner transcript management
- Compliance and audit liability

### Option 3: Independent Commercial Accreditation Partner

Partner with a commercial ACCME-accredited provider (Amedco, Pri-Med, CME Outfitters, etc.) instead of the universities.

- No university IP entanglements
- Faster setup, fewer stakeholders
- Typical revenue share: 10-15% (lower than academic institutions)
- Lose the Duke/UNC brand prestige

**Pros:** Speed, simplicity, lower revenue share
**Cons:** No institutional brand, less credibility, no existing relationship

### Recommendation

**Option 2 (Separate company + joint providership) is the recommended path.**

Rationale:
- IP remains fully owned by your company
- Faculty status provides built-in credibility and relationship access
- Joint providership is the standard commercial CME model — CME offices are familiar with it
- You can pursue dual accreditation (UNC for physicians, Duke for nurses) from a single company
- Revenue share is a cost of doing business that replaces the enormous cost of obtaining your own accreditation

## IP Considerations

**Critical: Review faculty agreements before proceeding.**

General guidelines (verify with an attorney):
- If built on your own time with your own resources → IP is likely yours
- If built using university resources, students, or directly related to faculty role → institution may claim ownership
- IP clauses vary by department and appointment type at both Duke and UNC

**Action item:** Consult an attorney who specializes in academic IP before pitching the CME offices. One consultation can prevent major complications later.

## Platform Technical Capabilities for CME

The platform already supports all ACCME/ANCC requirements:

| Requirement | Platform Feature |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Disclosure slides | Auto-generated per lesson with faculty COI, accreditation statements |
| Learning objectives | Displayed at start of each lesson, configurable per course |
| Assessment | Quizzes with passing thresholds per module |
| Evaluation | Post-activity evaluation (configurable) |
| Attestation | Learner attestation required before credit award |
| Multiple credit types | Supports dual accreditation (AMA PRA Cat 1 + ANCC) per course |
| Credit tracking | LifterLMS tracks completion, quiz scores, time spent |
| Expiration | Configurable credit expiration periods |
| Content delivery | Mobile app (iOS/Android), TV app (Apple TV, Fire TV, Google TV), web |

### Multi-Credit Course Example

A single course can be configured with:
```
Credits:
  - 3 AMA PRA Category 1 hours (UNC School of Medicine)
  - 3 ANCC Contact Hours (Duke University School of Nursing)
```

The disclosure slide automatically displays both accreditation statements and all available credit types.
